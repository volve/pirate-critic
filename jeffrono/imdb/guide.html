A SIMPLE BITTORRENT GUIDE

There are many ways of getting movies and TV these days.  You have a DVD player and cable TV.  You probably are one of the 6 million Netflix subscribers (I was one too) or one of the many millions of DVR or TiVo subscribers.  However, bit torrents is another way to get entertainment fast and easily.

** It’s also ILLEGAL to download copyrighted movies … this website is for EDUCATIONAL purposes only. **

If you have been living in a cave for the last few years you might not have heard of this phenomenon known as Bit Torrent (like a torrential storm of bits and bytes).  It is peer-to-peer software developed to leverage the use of each person’s individual bandwidth, such as your DSL or cable modem.  The idea is to download stuff (movies, music, TV, software, etc) from multiple people who have that stuff rather than from a single person.  When you do this, you get a torrent of data and can download stuff very quickly.  I’ve gotten speeds of up to 1 MB/sec – a full movie would download in about 11 minutes.  And I’m on the cheapest cable modem package.  You can basically download at your maximum bandwidth, which is pretty amazing.

Why should you care at all?  Well, virtually anything you can see or hear is available online: movies, TV shows, music. That means you could replace Netflix, Blockbuster, the movie theater, Tivo, your cable TV bill, and iTunes with a few mouse clicks:

1.    Software: Download uTorrent. Download and install the uTorrent application. This straightforward program lets you to download “torrents,” (small files named like “Pink_Floyd___Oh_By_The_Way_Box_Set_2007.torrent”) which point to the real file you want to download.  You don’t need to know why or how they work to get a lot out of this system.  Just know that you will open a “.torrent” file using the uTorrent program.

2.    Website: Find everything at BTJunkie, one of the best torrent sites on the net, which means you can literally find anything you want.  (But check out the top 10 sites for a better selection.) That new movie that just came out?  Sure.  Some band no one’s heard of?  Yeah.  And virtually any TV show or movie you can think of.

3.    IMDB Torrents: I created this site.  It is a mashup between the top rated movies on IMDB and BTJunkie.  I wrote a nifty perl script that went through thousands of movies and checked if torrents existed for them and confirmed their quality.  Crappy torrents were thrown out.  Go ahead – browse which movies are up and ready to be downloaded.

4.    TED (Torrent Episode Downloader): TiVo is soooo 2005.  Torrent Episode Downloader is an awesome little program that works just like TiVo, but for torrents.  Download the program and start adding your favorite TV shows.  Specify which episode you are up to and TED takes over from there.  It is fully integrated with the actual TV schedules so it knows when they are coming out and automatically checks RSS feeds from major bit torrent sites to locate and download new episodes as they become available.

5.    Speed Up: BitTorrent will run quite slowly until you make a quick tweak to your router. Before you go blaming your internet connection or your router on why your downloads are sluggish, you’ll need to forward all your bittorrent traffic directly to your computer.  This is called IP forwarding.  Don’t worry about the specifics.  All you need to know is this is a 5 minute ordeal, and will max out your download speeds: usually from about 10Kbytes/sec to 850Kbytes/sec – an enormous increase well worth your time.  Google “bittorrent IP forward router” with your router model.

6.    Security: Download PeerGuardian. You may be wondering if you can get caught downloading illegal files.  Yes, you can, but it is unlikely.  The way the RIAA or movie industry finds you is by tracking your IP address, which is the general location of your computer.  When you download a file from another computer (called a “peer”, as in P2P or peer-to-peer), you are telling that computer where to send its data.  If that peer is not a nerdy Lost fan in Ohio, but instead an RIAA drone computer whose purpose is to track and resolve IP addresses that download the new episode of Lost, then you may get screwed.  You’ll still be a long way from any legal action or fines, but it can happen.

So you want to protect yourself.  PeerGuardian is like a personal firewall built specifically to keep unwanted (read RIAA or MPAA) out by continually updating a list of known peer IP addresses that are “bad” (the ones that want to track you down for intellectual property infringement).  It completely blocks these addressees so you don’t unknowingly download anything from them.  In this way, you are virtually undetectable as long as you keep your wares to yourself and set PeerGuardian to automatically update.

7.    Watch: Download the best video software program, VLC Media Player.  This little program will play virtually any format of video you can download from the net, though most of the TV shows and movies from bittorrent sites are in DivX or XviD encoding.

8.    Listen: Download the best MP3 software, Media Monkey.  I’ve been a loyal Winamp user for about 10 years.  But it started to crash quite often, even after re-installing, which prompted me to find a new player.  Media Monkey blows Winamp away and virtually destroys iTunes in terms of functionality, ease, and innovation.  Trust me when I say it has drastically increased the efficiency of maintaining my ever-growing music collection.  My favorite feature is the automatic tagging of an album based on a search of Amazon’s website. It even downloads the cover art.
